What is the Dengue Fever?

Dengue Fever is a mosquito born viral infection which is transmitted through female mosquitos of the species aedes aegyptis. These mosquitos are also known to transmitting other dangerous viruses such as yellow fever and the zika virus. The Dengue Fever mainly occurs across tropical states,reports by the world health organization indicate that approximatley 390 million people per year are infected globablly from that approximatley 3.1 billion people from 128 countries are at risk of catching the Dengue Fever. Many Tropical Countries have befallen to this deadly epedemic a state that has seen a rise in the Dengue Fever is Sri Lanka.

How the Dengue Fever affects Sri Lanka Citizens?

There has been a massive outbreak of the Dengue Fever in Sri Lanka which occured in 2017 that reported a whopping 80732 cases including the death of 215 people. The outbreak is 4.3 fold higher than cases reported between 2010-2016. It has been reported that 43% of the cases reported from the western province with provinces such as Colombo and Gampha being affected the most. Most cases of the Dengue Fever occur between May-july during peak-monsoon season affecting 15 out of 25 districts and 60000 people.
